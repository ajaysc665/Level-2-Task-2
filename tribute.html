Ratan Tata

Entrepreneur, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons

Biography

Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group. He was born on December 28, 1937, in Mumbai, India. He received his undergraduate degree from Cornell University and a degree in Architecture from Harvard University.

Tata joined the Tata Group in 1962 and became the chairman of the company in 1991. During his tenure, he oversaw the acquisition of companies such as Tetley, Corus, and Jaguar Land Rover, expanding the group's global footprint. He retired as chairman in 2012, but remains active in business and philanthropic ventures.

Philanthropy

In addition to his business achievements, Ratan Tata is also known for his philanthropic work. He is the chairman of the Tata Trusts, one of India's oldest and largest public charitable organizations. The trust supports a variety of causes, including education, healthcare, and rural development.

Tata has also established the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, the National Centre for the Performing Arts, and the Tata Medical Center. He has received numerous awards for his philanthropy, including the Padma Vibhushan, one of India's highest civilian awards.
